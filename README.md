# Civilization Stack — Draft Domains

> **Status: working drafts.** These are exploratory domain drafts at varying stages of development, shared openly for feedback. They are not finished documents. The mature, completed work in this body of thought is the **Bounded Power Framework**, maintained separately (see below).

The Civilization Stack is an umbrella for a set of related framework domains. Each takes one slice of how power and infrastructure operate over people — and asks how that slice can be designed to stay accountable, interruptible, and replaceable when it fails.

All domains share one throughline:

> **Is this system structured to remain stoppable, expiring, reversible, visible, and harm-limited — or has it grown past the point where it can still be changed?**

The answer is allowed to differ in each domain. The question is not.

## The domains

Each is a single consolidated draft. Depth varies — some are extensive, others are earlier.

- **[Infrastructure](02-Infrastructure-DRAFT.md)** — Energy, water, transport, and communications kept modular, redundant, and repairable, so the physical layer stays maintainable instead of locked in.
- **[Economy](03-Economy-DRAFT.md)** — How provision is coordinated across strangers without letting economic power concentrate beyond reach.
- **[Data](04-Data-DRAFT.md)** — How information is collected, kept, and used so records serve the people they describe rather than becoming a permanent watch they cannot escape.
- **[Technology](05-Technology-DRAFT.md)** — How technological capability is chosen, deployed, and retired so tools stay tools people can still direct, not authorities no one chose.
- **[Governance](06-Governance-DRAFT.md)** — How governments can be structured so authority stays limited, renewable, auditable, and removable.
- **[Landscape-Transition](07-Landscape-Transition-DRAFT.md)** — Land use, settlement, transport, and food systems, and the long-horizon stewardship of the physical world for people who cannot vote yet.
- **[Civic-Units](08-Civic-Units-DRAFT.md)** — The human-scale built community where the whole Stack is applied at once — habitation, governance, work, services — designed so it can always be left.
- **[Education](09-Education-DRAFT.md)** — What education is for and how it stays bounded: core literacies, harm limits, renewal logic, and a lifelong learning record.
- **[Levity](10-Levity-DRAFT.md)** — Keeping a civilization able to laugh at itself, especially about its own power — protecting the cultural stop mechanism that no law restores once it is lost.

A fuller architecture map — how the layers rest on and bound one another, and the failure mode of each — is in **[00-INDEX.md](00-INDEX.md)**.

## Relationship to the Bounded Power Framework

The foundational layer is the **Bounded Power Framework (BPF)** — the meta-constraint on authority itself (expiration, stop mechanisms, renewal, containment) that governs every domain here and exempts none. BPF is the most developed part of this work and is maintained as its own repository under [WitnessMark](https://github.com/WitnessMark). These domains operationalize that same throughline within their own slice of civilization.

## Status and feedback

The Stack is in active development, maintained independently by one person, with AI assistance. It is not an incorporated organization. These drafts are shared to invite scrutiny — corrections, gaps, and hard questions are all welcome.

- Open a GitHub **issue** to raise a problem or a question.
- Open a **pull request** to suggest a change.
- Or email **WitnessMarkGitHub@proton.me**.

There is no submission fee and no editorial gatekeeping; feedback is reviewed informally and as time allows.

## Closing principle

> Civilization should become wiser and easier to sustain over time — not harder to repair.

The Civilization Stack is one attempt to put structural-design tools in the hands of ordinary people who live inside systems they did not design.
